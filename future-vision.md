## Elliot Hershberg's Vision (Century of Bio)

A concrete scenario for near-future personalized oncology:

### Step 1: Early Detection
Patient receives positive cancer screening (liquid biopsy, imaging, or clinical finding).

### Step 2: Consumer Concierge Oncology Platform
- Enrollment cost: ~$1,499 initial workup
- AI agents analyze medical history
- Order refined diagnostics based on cancer type

### Step 3: AI-Guided Diagnostics
- Comprehensive sequencing (WGS, RNA-seq, scRNA-seq)
- Bioinformatics analysis automated by AI
- Target identification (like FAP in Sid's case)
- Treatment matching against global database

### Step 4: Platform Therapeutics
- Checkpoint inhibitors (now generic and cheap)
- Off-the-shelf neoantigen vaccines (mRNA platform)
- Custom radioligand therapies (swap targeting molecule)
- CAR-T or engineered cell therapies as needed

### Step 5: Real-Time Monitoring
- ctDNA tracking (weekly or biweekly)
- AI interprets trends
- Treatment adjustments in real-time

### Estimated Total Cost: ~$175,000
(vs. $250,000+ for standard pancreatic cancer treatment)

## Platform Technologies That Enable Scaling

### mRNA Vaccine Platforms
- **Principle**: Same manufacturing process, different antigen payload
- **Example**: Moderna's personalized cancer vaccine (mRNA-4157/V940) cut melanoma recurrence by ~50% in trials
- **Scaling**: Once the platform is validated, adding new tumor-specific antigens is incremental cost
- **Timeline**: Neoantigen prediction → mRNA synthesis → quality control → patient dosing in weeks, not years

### Radioligand Therapy Platforms
- **Principle**: Same isotope chemistry, different targeting ligand
- **Example**: 177Lu-PSMA (Pluvicto) for prostate cancer → same approach extended to FAP, B7H3, EphA2
- **Scaling**: New targets identified by transcriptomics, then conjugated to existing isotope delivery
- **Theranostic**: Same ligand used for diagnosis (68Ga) and therapy (177Lu/225Ac)

### Cell Therapy Platforms
- **CAR-T**: Engineer patient's T cells to target specific tumor antigens
- **CAR-NK**: Off-the-shelf NK cell therapies (no patient-specific manufacturing)
- **Logic gates**: Genetic circuits that activate only when multiple tumor signals are present (Sid's "nuclear option" backup)

### Single-Cell Sequencing
- **10x Genomics**: Dominant platform, costs dropping
- **Spatial transcriptomics**: Map gene expression in tissue context
- **Enables**: Discovery of non-obvious targets (like FAP in osteosarcoma)

### AI/ML Integration
- Bioinformatics automation
- Treatment recommendation engines
- Real-time ctDNA interpretation
- Clinical trial matching
- Drug interaction checking

## Regulatory Evolution

### FDA Progress
- **Individual Patient Expanded Access (Form 3926)**: Already works — 48hr approval
- **"Plausible mechanism" pathways**: Proposed for personalized therapies
- **Platform technology frameworks**: Regulatory structure for therapies that share a platform but differ in payload

### What's Needed
- Hospital IRB modernization to match FDA speed
- Standardized tissue cryopreservation protocols
- Patient data portability standards (like Open Banking, but for health)
- Insurance models that cover personalized therapies
- Tort reform to protect physicians supporting unconventional approaches

## The William Gibson Quote

> "The future is already here — it's just not evenly distributed."

Every technology used in Sid's treatment exists today:
- scRNA-seq: commercially available
- Neoantigen vaccines: in clinical trials and expanded access
- Radioligand therapy: FDA-approved for some cancers, expandable to others
- Checkpoint inhibitors: FDA-approved, approaching generic pricing
- ctDNA monitoring: commercially available from multiple providers
- AI analysis: available via ChatGPT, Claude, and specialized biomedical AI

The gap is not technology. It's access, coordination, and willingness to operate outside the standard-of-care paradigm.
