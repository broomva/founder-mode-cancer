# Key Breakthroughs and Turning Points

## 1. Single-Cell RNA-seq Reveals FAP Overexpression

**When**: Analysis of scRNA-seq data (2024)
**What**: Standard genomic panels showed nothing actionable beyond the known mutations. But single-cell RNA sequencing of tumor tissue revealed that cancer cells were overexpressing fibroblast activation protein (FAP) — a marker of the wound-healing pathway that tumors hijack to build protective stroma.

**Why it mattered**: FAP is targetable. It's not a standard clinical biomarker for osteosarcoma. Without research-grade transcriptomics, this would never have been discovered.

**What it enabled**: The entire radioligand therapy arm of treatment.

---

## 2. 68Ga-FAP PET Confirms the Target

**When**: October 23, 2024
**What**: A PET scan using gallium-68 conjugated to a FAP-targeting ligand. The tumor lit up — confirming high FAP expression in vivo.

**Why it mattered**: This is the "theranostic" principle — use the same targeting molecule for diagnosis (68Ga for imaging) and therapy (177Lu or 225Ac for radiation). Positive imaging = green light for treatment.

---

## 3. Radioligand Therapy Shrinks the Tumor

**When**: February - June 2025
**What**: Three doses of FAP-targeted radioligand therapy:
- 177Lu-FAPi (Feb 2025): Beta emitter, longer range
- 225Ac-FAPi (Mar 2025): Alpha emitter, more potent, shorter range
- 225Ac-FAPi (Jun 2025): Second alpha dose

**Why it mattered**: The tumor shrank enough to become surgically resectable again. Without this, the second surgery would not have been possible.

**Context**: This therapy was accessed in Germany. Not available through standard US oncology channels. Required expanded access.

---

## 4. Immune Microenvironment Transformation

**When**: Confirmed at second surgery, April 17, 2025 (MSKCC)
**What**: Fresh tissue analysis showed:
- Before combined treatment: **19% T cells** among tumor-infiltrating immune cells
- After treatment: **89% T cells**

**Why it mattered**: This is the holy grail of cancer immunotherapy — converting a "cold" tumor (immune-excluded) to a "hot" tumor (immune-infiltrated). The parallel multi-modal approach succeeded where individual therapies typically fail in osteosarcoma.

**What contributed**:
- Checkpoint inhibitors (Dostarlimab + Ipilimumab): removed immune brakes
- Neoantigen vaccines (JLF + CeGaT): trained T cells to recognize tumor
- Oncolytic virus (AdaPT-001): killed tumor cells, released antigens, trapped TGF-beta
- NK cells (SNK-01): innate killing
- Radioligand: direct cell killing, antigen release
- The synergy of all of these together produced the transformation

---

## 5. ctDNA Drops to Undetectable

**When**: Mid-2024, within ~2 months of starting combined therapy
**What**: Signatera ctDNA went from 2.46 MTM/mL (recurrence) to 0 (undetectable). Personalis went from 963 PPM to not detected.

**Why it mattered**: Liquid biopsy provided real-time confirmation that the treatment combination was working — weeks before imaging could show tumor shrinkage. This prevented the standard oncology pattern of "wait 3 months, scan, adjust."

---

## 6. FDA Expanded Access Works

**When**: Throughout 2024-2025
**What**: Every Individual Patient Expanded Access IND (FDA Form 3926) was approved within 48 hours.

**Why it mattered**: Contradicts the narrative that the FDA is the primary barrier to experimental treatment. The real bottleneck was hospital IRBs and institutional processes, not federal regulators.

---

## 7. The Cost Insight

> "It costs $1B to get a drug approved. But it costs $1M to dose a single person with personalized therapy."

**Implication**: The economics of personalized medicine are fundamentally different from blockbuster drug development. A $1M personalized treatment is expensive but within reach of insurance or specialized financing. The gap between population medicine and personalized medicine is narrowing as platform technologies (mRNA vaccines, radioligand conjugation, single-cell sequencing) become more accessible.

## What Made the Difference: A Summary

| Factor | Standard Care | Founder Mode |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Diagnostics | Gene panel + basic WES | WGS + WES + scRNA-seq + ctDNA x3 + organoids + PET with novel tracers |
| Treatment sequence | Sequential (fail → next) | Parallel (layer compatible therapies) |
| Treatment access | Clinical trials or approved drugs | Expanded access IND (48hr approval) |
| Data ownership | Hospital retains, reports summary | Patient owns, shares publicly (25TB) |
| Decision maker | Physician recommends | Patient drives strategy with expert advisors |
| Monitoring | Scans every 3 months | ctDNA every 2-4 weeks + serial scRNA-seq |
| Tissue handling | Formalin-fixed (destroys RNA) | Cryopreserved (preserves all molecular info) |
