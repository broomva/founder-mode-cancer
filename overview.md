# Case Overview

## Patient

**Sid Sijbrandij** — co-founder and CEO of GitLab. Built a fully remote company of 2,500+ employees. Age 45 at diagnosis.

## Diagnosis

- **Cancer**: Osteosarcoma (bone cancer)
- **Location**: T5 vertebra (upper thoracic spine)
- **Tumor size**: 6 cm mass at discovery
- **Date**: November 10, 2022
- **Rarity**: Osteosarcoma is uncommon in adults; typically a pediatric cancer

## Standard-of-Care Phase (2022-2024)

1. **Surgery** (Dec 2022): Removal of T4/T5 vertebrae with titanium fusion
2. **Radiation** (Jan-Feb 2023): Stereotactic body radiotherapy (SBRT) to thoracic spine
3. **Chemotherapy** (Feb-Jun 2023): Cisplatin + Doxorubicin (4 cycles), then Doxorubicin + Ifosfamide (2 cycles). Required 4 blood transfusions. Described as "devastating."
4. **Remission**: Achieved after standard treatment
5. **Recurrence** (2024): Cancer returned. Standard options exhausted.

## Founder Mode Phase (2024-present)

Upon recurrence, Sid rejected the passive patient role. Instead of searching for a single clinical trial, he:

- Recruited a dedicated care team (Jacob Stern from 10x Genomics as care CEO)
- Pursued every available diagnostic modality simultaneously
- Developed personalized treatments via FDA expanded access (Form 3926)
- Ran therapies in parallel rather than sequentially
- Open-sourced all his data (25TB on Google Cloud)

## Outcome

- **Immune infiltration**: T cells went from 19% to 89% of tumor-infiltrating immune cells
- **Tumor response**: Shrank enough for second surgery (April 2025)
- **MRD status**: Cancer undetectable as of early 2026
- **Ongoing**: Preventive mRNA neoantigen vaccine to sustain immune memory
