# Secondary Conditions and Complications

Cancer treatment is not just about the tumor. Sid's journey involved significant complications from treatment side effects, each requiring their own management.

---

## 1. Avascular Necrosis (AVN) of the Hip

### Cause
Likely steroid-induced (dexamethasone used multiple times for inflammation management) and/or related to XGeva (denosumab) treatment.

### Timeline
- **Oct 2025**: Hip pain begins
- **Dec 2025**: Bone survey
- **Jan 2026**: MRI hips
- **Mar 2026**: MRI jaw (checking for jaw osteonecrosis — known risk of denosumab)
- **Mar 2026**: Bone survey

### Diagnostics
- DXA scan (bone density): Mar 2025
- MRI hips: Jan 2026
- MRI jaw: Mar 2026
- Bone surveys: Dec 2025, Mar 2026

### Treatment
- **Mar 12, 2026**: Core decompression + BMAC (bone marrow aspirate concentrate) + PRP (platelet-rich plasma)
- This is a joint-preserving procedure for early AVN — drilling into the necrotic bone to restore blood flow, augmented with growth factors

## 2. Thyroid Dysfunction (Hyperthyroidism)

### Cause
Likely checkpoint inhibitor-induced thyroiditis. Dostarlimab and Ipilimumab are known to cause autoimmune thyroid dysfunction in a significant percentage of patients.

### Timeline
- **Jan 2025**: Thyroid ultrasound
- **Jun 2025 - Mar 2026**: Methimazole treatment (9 months)

### Significance
Checkpoint inhibitor-related thyroid dysfunction is actually a positive prognostic sign — it indicates the immune system is activated. Patients who develop immune-related adverse events (irAEs) from checkpoint inhibitors tend to have better tumor responses.

## 3. Spinal Surgical Site Infection / Seroma

### Context
After two major spinal surgeries (Dec 2022 at UCSF, Apr 2025 at MSKCC) with titanium hardware, post-surgical complications developed.

### Timeline
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Aug 2025 | Amoxicillin started (prophylaxis) |
| Sep 2025 | Seroma drainage #1 — cultures: rare staph epidermis, staph haemolyticus, cutibacterium |
| Sep 2025 | Antibiotics: Bactrim, Amoxicillin, Levofloxacin, Ciprofloxacin |
| Sep 2025 | Dalbavancin (long-acting IV antibiotic, dose 1) |
| Sep 2025 | Dalbavancin dose 2 |
| Oct 2025 | Seroma drainage #2 — cultures negative |
| Oct 2025 | Pathology: paucicellular fibroadipose tissue, no tumor, no significant inflammation |
| Nov 2025 | Dalbavancin dose 3 |
| Nov 2025 | Hardware removal (titanium) |
| Nov 2025 | Seroma drainage #3 — benign, increased eosinophils |
| Nov 2025 | CSF patch (cerebrospinal fluid leak repair) |
| Nov 2025 | Dalbavancin dose 4 |
| Jan 2026 | Dalbavancin dose 5 (final) |

### Antibiotic Summary
| Drug | Duration | Route |
|------|----------|-------|
| Amoxicillin | Aug-Sep 2025 | Oral |
| Bactrim | Sep 2025 (12 days) | Oral |
| Levofloxacin | Sep 2025 (16 days) | Oral |
| Ciprofloxacin | Sep 2025 (3 days) | Oral |
| Dalbavancin | Sep 2025 - Jan 2026 (5 doses) | IV (long-acting) |

### Pathology Results
- **Sep 2025**: Organizing hematoma/seroma with focal granulation tissue and prior procedure site changes
- **Oct 2025**: Paucicellular fibroadipose tissue, no tumor or significant inflammation
- **Nov 2025**: Benign, increased eosinophils; negative for malignancy; cultures negative

### Resolution
Hardware removal in November 2025 followed by ongoing Dalbavancin therapy appears to have resolved the infection. No further drainage needed after November 2025.

---

## Key Takeaway

Aggressive cancer treatment comes with significant collateral damage. Managing complications is an integral part of the treatment journey:
- Checkpoint inhibitors → autoimmune thyroiditis (managed with methimazole)
- Steroids → avascular necrosis of hip (managed with core decompression)
- Spinal surgery → infection/seroma (managed with hardware removal + IV antibiotics)

Each complication required its own diagnostic workup, specialist consultation, and treatment protocol — adding complexity to an already complex care plan.
